3D Printers

3D printers are machines that create objects layer by layer from digital designs. Instead of cutting or shaping material, they build things from the ground up, often using plastics, resins, or even metals.

Types of 3D Printers

  • FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling): The most common type, using melted plastic filament.
  • SLA (Stereolithography): Uses liquid resin cured with UV light for detailed prints.
  • SLS (Selective Laser Sintering): Fuses powdered material with a laser, often used for durable prototypes.

What Can You Make?

  • Prototypes: Test product designs quickly and affordably.
  • Custom Parts: Replace broken pieces or create unique fittings.
  • Models & Art: From miniatures to sculptures, creativity has no limits.

Why Use It?

3D printing is perfect for rapid prototyping, customization, and small-scale production without expensive tooling.
